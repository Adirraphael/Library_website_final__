About the UW Madison Library

Since our founding in 1848, the University of Wisconsin-Madison Libraries have grown into one of North America’s premier research library systems, housing over 11 million volumes and a vast collection of digital resources across multiple branches. Each of our libraries serves a unique purpose, from the expansive humanities and social sciences collections in Memorial Library to the specialized focus on agricultural and life sciences at Steenbock Library. At UW-Madison Libraries, we are dedicated to supporting the academic excellence and innovative research of our students, faculty, and staff. Our mission is to provide equitable access to information, foster an inclusive learning environment, and offer resources that support both individual and collaborative pursuits. We are proud to uphold and preserve historical records and cultural heritage, particularly focusing on Wisconsin’s contributions to academia and society. As technology evolves, so do we. Our digital offerings continue to expand, allowing the UW-Madison community to access a wide array of resources—from our comprehensive online catalog to research databases, e-books, and multimedia collections—anytime and anywhere. We also host a variety of events, workshops, and lectures, fostering community engagement and lifelong learning for both our campus and the public. Whether you’re finding a quiet space to study, conducting groundbreaking research, or exploring our archives, we are here to be a vital partner in your academic journey. At UW-Madison Libraries, we are committed to enriching the intellectual life of our community and nurturing a lifelong love for learning.
